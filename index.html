Model Assumptions & Caveats

What this tool does

A first-order, single-stage rocket model for comparing transportation architectures. Useful for identifying which routes have inherent delta-v and mass advantages. Not suitable for mission design.

Simplifications & limitations

  • No staging model. Real vehicles for high-Δv routes (>6 km/s) would use staging, which dramatically reduces initial mass. The single-stage mass ratios here are conservative.
  • No structural/dry mass. mⁿ is treated as pure payload. In reality, tanks and structure add ~5–15% of propellant mass to mⁿ.
  • Δv values are Hohmann-transfer approximations at mean Earth–Mars distance. Actual values vary ±15–20% depending on launch window alignment.
  • Aerobraking is massless in this model. Heat shields add mass (typically 10–20% of entry mass) not captured here.
  • Cost model is highly simplified: initial mass × $/kg at origin. Does not model propellant sourcing chain, infrastructure, or operations costs separately.
  • Mars/Deimos-origin routes use the ISRU propellant cost (default $0) for propellant. This assumes propellant is pre-positioned; the actual cost depends on the delivery architecture.
  • LLO→TMI uses patched-conic approximation (~1.6 km/s). This is much less than LEO→TMI because LLO already sits high in Earth’s gravity well — the core argument for using lunar-derived propellant.
  • Lunar refueling cost model (when enabled): Earth launches only the vehicle + ascent propellant; in-space propellant comes from the Moon at the lunar-prop cost. Cost = (m₀_launch × $/kg_LEO) + (m_prop_in_space × $/kg_lunar_prop). Does not model the lunar prop delivery chain itself.

Δ-v reference values

  • Earth surface → LEO: ~9.4 km/s (includes ~1.5 km/s gravity and drag losses)
  • LEO → TMI: ~3.6 km/s (Hohmann, Oberth effect from LEO)
  • LLO → TMI: ~1.6 km/s (patched-conic; the chief lunar-launch advantage)
  • Mars aerocapture to LMO: ~0.9 km/s; propulsive: ~2.5 km/s
  • LMO (300 km) ↔ Deimos (23,460 km): ~1.7 km/s (Hohmann)
  • Deimos → TEI: ~1.9 km/s to achieve C3 ≈ 7 km²/s² for Earth return
  • Mars surface → LMO: ~3.8 km/s

Purpose

This tool is designed for comparative trade studies only. The primary question is whether Deimos provides logistical leverage as a staging node relative to direct Earth–Mars transport. Compare routes relative to each other; do not treat absolute values as precise mission parameters.

Key insight to watch for

Deimos sits near the top of Mars’ gravity well and requires only ~1.7 km/s to reach from low Mars orbit. The question is whether pre-positioning propellant or cargo at Deimos reduces the overall mission mass budget for subsequent surface operations, even accounting for the extra Δv to reach Deimos first.
